## PART 1 — INOCULATION & PREBUNKING SCIENCE

The scientific backbone of the product. "Inoculation theory" (McGuire, 1960s) holds that exposing people to a *weakened* dose of a manipulation technique, plus a refutation, builds psychological resistance — like a vaccine. "Prebunking" is the applied version. This is the most evidence-backed cognitive-defense paradigm available.

### 1.1 Cambridge Social Decision-Making Lab (SDMLab) — the research home

- **Who:** Prof. Sander van der Linden (Director) and Dr. Jon Roozenbeek, Dept. of Psychology, University of Cambridge.
- **URL:** https://www.sdmlab.psychol.cam.ac.uk/ and the Bad News research page https://www.sdmlab.psychol.cam.ac.uk/research/bad-news-game
- **What it offers:** The canonical "technique-based" / "broad-spectrum" inoculation paradigm — teach the *technique* of manipulation (not each individual false claim), so resistance generalizes to never-before-seen misinformation. This is the core design principle for our whole curriculum.
- **Adapt into modules:** Build the product's spine as "learn the manipulation technique, not the individual hoax." Each technique = one module (see the technique taxonomy in 1.2 and 1.3).
- **License/usability:** 🔴 Lab site is reference; the *games and videos* it produced have their own terms (below). Cite van der Linden & Roozenbeek as the theory source.

### 1.2 "Bad News" game (Roozenbeek & van der Linden, with DROG)

- **What it is:** A free browser game where the player role-plays a disinformation creator, learning **six manipulation techniques** by using them in a simulated social feed:
  1. **Impersonation** (impersonating people/orgs, e.g. fake "doctors" or official accounts)
  2. **Emotion** (emotionally manipulative language, esp. appeals to fear/outrage)
  3. **Polarization** ("us vs. them" framing to drive groups apart)
  4. **Conspiracy** (floating conspiracy theories)
  5. **Discrediting** (attacking/denying credible opponents, sowing doubt)
  6. **Trolling** (provoking and manipulating discourse for reaction)
- **Play it:** https://www.getbadnews.com (also a junior version, and themed spin-offs Go Viral! and Harmony Square).
- **Key DATA — foundational study:** Roozenbeek, J., & van der Linden, S. (2019). "Fake news game confers psychological resistance against online misinformation." *Palgrave Communications*, 5:65. Finding: playing the game significantly reduced perceived reliability/persuasiveness of manipulative content; players got better at spotting techniques. DOI: 10.1057/s41599-019-0279-9.
- **Key DATA — cross-cultural replication (critical for our Arabic ambition):** Roozenbeek, J., van der Linden, S., & Nygren, T. (2020). "Prebunking interventions based on 'inoculation' theory can reduce susceptibility to misinformation across cultures." *HKS Misinformation Review*. Tested in Swedish, German, Greek, Polish (total **N ≈ 5,061**). Effect sizes (Cohen's d): **Germany d=0.41, Greece d=0.36, Poland d=0.33, Sweden d=0.24** — "significant and meaningful reductions in the perceived reliability of manipulative content across all languages," largely independent of demographics. DOI: 10.37016//mr-2020-008. URL: https://misinforeview.hks.harvard.edu/article/global-vaccination-badnews/
  - **NOTE / GAP:** No Arabic or MENA language was in this set. This is a documented research gap and our opportunity — an Arabic-validated prebunking instrument would be novel.
- **Adapt into modules:** Our six core "deception technique" lessons can map almost 1:1 to these six, recast with Egyptian/Arabic examples. The role-play ("become the manipulator for 5 minutes") is the single most-validated active-learning mechanic — replicate it.
- **License/usability:** 🟡 Game is free to play and widely used in classrooms; it is *not* an open-source asset to rehost. For our own build we re-implement the *paradigm* (which is public science) with original Arabic content, and we can *link to* the official game. Confirm any direct-embed permission with the Cambridge team / DROG.

### 1.3 Google Jigsaw × Cambridge × Bristol — "Inoculation Science" prebunking videos (Truth Labs for Education)

- **What it is:** Short (<2 min) animated videos that prebunk a single manipulation tactic/logical fallacy using movie/TV clips (technique-focused, not politics-specific). Hub lists ~11 videos:
  Emotional Language · Incoherence · False Dichotomies · Scapegoating · Ad Hominem · Polarisation · Conspiracy Theories · Fake Experts · Whataboutism · Moving the Goalposts · Straw Man.
- **Hub / watch:** https://inoculation.science/ and https://inoculation.science/inoculation-videos/
- **Key DATA — landmark field study:** Roozenbeek, J., van der Linden, S., et al. (2022). "Psychological inoculation improves resilience against misinformation on social media." *Science Advances*, 8(34), eabo6254. Seven experiments, **~30,000 participants** total; plus a real-world YouTube ad deployment: **~5.4 million** users exposed, **~1 million** watched ≥30s, **22,632** answered a test question — recognition of manipulation techniques rose **~5% on average** in the field; lab effects larger (e.g., participants ~**1.95×** better at spotting false dichotomies, ~**2.14×** better at spotting incoherence). Cost ≈ **$0.05/effective view**. DOI: 10.1126/sciadv.abo6254.
- **Newer validated set:** Biddlestone, M., et al. (2025). "Tune in to the prebunking network! Development and validation of six inoculation videos…" *Political Psychology*. https://onlinelibrary.wiley.com/doi/full/10.1111/pops.70015
- **Adapt into modules:** This is our **format template** for short-form video: forewarning → weakened dose of the tactic → refutation → "now spot it." The <2-min, clip-illustrated, technique-named format is proven and cheap to scale. Produce Arabic-dubbed/subtitled originals in this mold.
- **License/usability:** 🟡 Videos are provided for educational/public use via Truth Labs for Education; no blanket open license is posted on the video page — **request explicit permission** to redistribute/subtitle, or (safer) produce original Arabic videos using the same published framework. The *method* is open science (cite Science Advances).

### 1.4 Google Jigsaw — "A Practical Guide to Prebunking Misinformation"

- **Who:** Jigsaw (Google) with University of Cambridge and BBC Media Action.
- **URL (PDF):** https://prebunking.withgoogle.com/docs/A_Practical_Guide_to_Prebunking_Misinformation.pdf
- **What it offers — directly trainable guidance.** A campaign-design playbook distinguishing **three prebunking modes**:
  - **Fact-based** — pre-emptively counter a *specific* false claim with evidence.
  - **Logic-based** — teach the *manipulation technique / fallacy* so resistance generalizes (this is the scalable one we lead with).
  - **Source-based** — build calibrated skepticism toward unreliable sources.
  Plus a 5-step build loop: (1) identify target misconceptions via audience research → (2) pick the prebunking mode for the threat → (3) design evidence-based, emotionally resonant messaging → (4) test/refine with the target audience → (5) deploy & monitor.
- **Adapt into modules:** Use the three-mode taxonomy as our internal content-design rubric; use the 5-step loop as the SOP for producing each new training unit. The "logic-based scales best" finding justifies a technique-first curriculum.
- **License/usability:** 🟡 Public practitioner guide; cite Jigsaw/Cambridge/BBC. Treat as guidance to *follow*, not content to *republish*.

### 1.5 Prebunking meta-analyses & evidence syntheses (the honest evidence base)

Use these to keep our claims calibrated (One-Law). The picture is positive but nuanced.

- **Lu, C., Hu, B., Li, Q., Bi, C., & Ju, X.-D. (2023).** "Psychological Inoculation for Credibility Assessment, Sharing Intention, and Discernment of Misinformation: Systematic Review and Meta-Analysis." *J Med Internet Res*, 25:e49255. **42 studies, N=42,530.** Effects: misinformation credibility **d=−0.36** (95% CI −0.50 to −0.23); real-info credibility **d=+0.20**; **credibility discernment d=+0.20**; **sharing discernment d=+0.18**. Note: misinformation-*sharing* reduction was not significant (d=−0.35, CI crosses 0). DOI: 10.2196/49255. 🟢 **CC BY** (open access — quotable/figures reusable with attribution). URL: https://www.jmir.org/2023/1/e49255
- **Signal-detection-theory caveat (important for intellectual honesty):** A 2025 signal-detection meta-analysis (33 inoculation experiments, N≈37,075) found inoculation improves genuine *discernment* without inducing bias — but a *separate* network meta-analysis (30 experiments, N≈30,530) found some prebunking mainly shifted *response criterion* (people just say "false" more often) rather than improving true discrimination. **Lesson for us:** design assessments that separate "skepticism" from "accuracy," and avoid over-claiming. URLs: https://www.sciencedirect.com/science/article/pii/S2352250X25002076 and https://www.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/pmc/articles/PMC12428163/
- **Traberg, C. S., Roozenbeek, J., & van der Linden, S. (2022).** "Psychological Inoculation against Misinformation: Current Evidence and Future Directions." *The ANNALS of the AAPSS*, 700(1). State-of-the-field review. https://journals.sagepub.com/doi/full/10.1177/00027162221087936
- **Adapt into modules:** These give us defensible, citable effect-size language for marketing and for an internal "evidence dossier." Also a design mandate: build *discernment* (truth AND falsehood), not blanket distrust.
- **License/usability:** Mixed — JMIR is 🟢 CC BY; others 🔴 cite-only. Always cite.

### 1.6 The games as a literature — independent reviews & replications

- Basol, M., Roozenbeek, J., & van der Linden, S. (2020). "Good News about Bad News: Gamified Inoculation Boosts Confidence and Cognitive Immunity Against Fake News." *Journal of Cognition*, 4(1):91. 🟢 open. https://journalofcognition.org/articles/10.5334/joc.91
- Breaking Harmony Square study (political-misinformation game): *HKS Misinformation Review.* https://misinforeview.hks.harvard.edu/article/breaking-harmony-square-a-game-that-inoculates-against-political-misinformation/ 🟢 CC BY.
- Critical replication (multimodal): "How to 'inoculate' against multimodal misinformation: A conceptual replication of Roozenbeek and van der Linden (2020)." *Scientific Reports*, 2023. https://www.nature.com/articles/s41598-023-43885-2 🟢 open.
- **Adapt into modules:** Use confidence-building findings (Basol et al.) — prebunking raises *confidence in one's own judgment*, not just accuracy. Bake a confidence/metacognition check into each module.

## PART 2 — MEDIA-LITERACY CURRICULA

Ready-made pedagogy, lesson structures, and competency frameworks we can localize.

Modular, with pedagogy suggestions for educators and learners. ### 2.4 IREX — "Learn to Discern" (L2D)

- **What it offers:** A field-proven adult + in-school media-literacy methodology. Distinctive features beyond standard MIL: builds **emotional intelligence and empathy**, trains people to **detect manipulation in their own emotional reactions**, and explicitly coaches participants to **pass the skills on to family/friends** (social diffusion). Versions: L2D (community/adult) and **L2D in Education (L2D-Ed)** for schools.
- **URLs:** project https://www.irex.org/project/learn-discern-l2d-media-literacy-training · L2D-Ed https://www.irex.org/project/strengthening-media-literacy-ukrainian-education-system
- **Key DATA:** Murrock, Gould, Babayev, et al. — Ukraine impact evaluation (2017, quasi-experimental, stratified random sample, **N=412**): L2D participants showed significantly better news-analysis skills, greater knowledge of the media environment, stronger media-consumption agency, and were more likely to identify disinformation and separate fact from opinion. **Skills persisted ~1.5 years later** — unusual; most campaigns' effects wane within a year. Peer-reviewed write-up: Murrock et al., "Winning the War on State-Sponsored Propaganda…" *Journal of Media Literacy Education*, 10(2). https://digitalcommons.uri.edu/jmle/vol10/iss2/4/
- **Adapt into modules:** Adopt the **emotion-as-a-detector** lesson (notice your own outrage → pause → check) and the **teach-it-forward** mechanic (every learner gets a "now explain this to one relative" task). The 1.5-yr persistence finding supports our "habit-formation" framing.
- **License/usability:** 🟡 Methodology is documented publicly; full L2D curricula are IREX-owned (often shared via partnership/training-of-trainers). The *peer-reviewed evaluation* (JMLE) is 🟢 open. Engage IREX for curriculum licensing; cite the JMLE study freely.

### 2.5 Stanford SHEG → Digital Inquiry Group (DIG) — Civic Online Reasoning (COR)

- **What it offers:** Free, classroom-ready lessons + assessments teaching students to evaluate online information. Now hosted by the **Digital Inquiry Group** (spun out of Stanford History Education Group). Built on **three core questions**: (1) *Who's behind the information?* (2) *What's the evidence?* (3) *What do other sources say?* Flagship skill = **lateral reading** (leave the page; open new tabs to check what trusted sources say about the source) — the evidence-based habit professional fact-checkers use.
- **Lessons include:** Intro to Lateral Reading; Lateral Reading Resources & Practice; Lateral Reading with News Stories; Lateral Reading with Fact-Checking Organizations; "Click Restraint," etc. Download single lessons or the full curriculum.
- **URLs:** https://cor.stanford.edu/ and https://inquirygroup.org/ (curriculum at https://cor.inquirygroup.org/curriculum)
- **Supporting research:** Wineburg & McGrew studies showing professional fact-checkers (who read laterally) outperform historians and students at judging source credibility; basis of the COR design.
- **Adapt into modules:** **Lateral reading is our single highest-leverage skill module** — concrete, teachable, transferable to Arabic web sources. Recreate "Who's behind it / What's the evidence / What do others say?" as a repeatable 3-question reflex with Egyptian examples (local news sites, viral WhatsApp/Facebook posts).
- **License/usability:** 🟡 Free for educational use; download requires a free account/registration. Materials are generally free to use in teaching — confirm DIG's current terms for adaptation/translation before publishing Arabic derivatives.

### 2.6 First Draft (and the "Information Disorder" framework, Claire Wardle)

- **What it offers:** The widely adopted **typology of information disorder** — the shared vocabulary for the whole field:
  - **3 types:** *Misinformation* (false, shared without intent to harm) · *Disinformation* (false, shared to harm) · *Malinformation* (true info weaponized to harm).
  - **7 forms:** satire/parody · false connection · misleading content · false context · imposter content · manipulated content · fabricated content.
- **Source:** Wardle, C., & Derakhshan, H. (2017). *Information Disorder: Toward an Interdisciplinary Framework for Research and Policymaking.* Council of Europe. https://shorensteincenter.org/resource/information-disorder-framework-for-research-and-policymaking/ · First Draft explainer https://firstdraftnews.org/long-form-article/understanding-information-disorder/ · useful graphics https://medium.com/1st-draft/information-disorder-part-3-useful-graphics-2446c7dbb485
- **Adapt into modules:** Use the 3-types / 7-forms as our **foundational taxonomy module** (lesson 1: "name the thing you're looking at"). Translate the 7 forms into clean Arabic terms — this becomes shared product vocabulary. Note First Draft closed in 2022; its resources live on via archives, the Council of Europe report, and successor work at the Brown School of Public Health (Information Futures Lab).
- **License/usability:** 🟡/🟢 Council of Europe report is freely distributed; First Draft training resources were CC-friendly but the org is defunct — cite the report and Wardle as canonical; reproduce graphics only with attribution.

### 2.7 SIFT / "Four Moves" (Mike Caulfield)

- **What it offers:** The most teachable lightweight verification reflex for the general public: **Stop · Investigate the source · Find better coverage · Trace claims/quotes/media to the original context.** Pairs with lateral reading. Caulfield & Wineburg's book *Verified* (2023) is the long-form version.
- **URL:** Caulfield's "Four Moves" / SIFT (e.g., https://hapgood.us/ and many library guides) — note widely mirrored.
- **Adapt into modules:** SIFT is our **"30-second gut-check" micro-module** for non-expert users — easy to memorize, ideal for short-form/social delivery and for an Arabic mnemonic. (Design an Arabic-acronym equivalent so it sticks.)
- **License/usability:** 🟢 **CC BY 4.0** — Caulfield explicitly licenses SIFT materials CC BY 4.0; freely adaptable/translatable with attribution. (Confirmed on derived guides, e.g. Barton CC library PDF: "Adapted from Mike Caulfield's materials with a CC BY 4.0 license.") **This is one of our most reuse-friendly assets.**

## PART 3 — SOURCE, FACT-CHECK & EVIDENCE STANDARDS

The rules that make our content credible and that we teach users to expect from *good* sources.

### 3.1 IFCN Code of Principles (Poynter)

- **What it offers:** The global gold standard for what a trustworthy fact-checker does — **five commitments**: (1) **Nonpartisanship & Fairness** (same standard/process for every check; don't pick sides); (2) **Transparency of Sources** (readers can replicate the check); (3) **Transparency of Funding & Organization**; (4) **Transparency of Methodology**; (5) **Open & Honest Corrections** (published, followed-through corrections policy). Applicants assessed against **31 criteria** by independent assessors + advisory board.
- **URLs:** https://www.ifcncodeofprinciples.poynter.org/ and the commitments https://ifcncodeofprinciples.poynter.org/the-commitments
- **Adapt into modules:** Two uses — (a) **our own editorial standard** (we publicly adhere to IFCN-style transparency/corrections, which earns trust in a low-trust Egyptian media climate); (b) a **"how to vet a fact-checker" lesson** — teach users the 5 commitments as a checklist for judging *any* outlet claiming to debunk.
- **License/usability:** 🔴 Cite-only (it's a standard, not content). Becoming a verified signatory is a credibility play worth considering for the org.

### 3.2 WHO — Infodemic Management Framework

- **What it offers:** A public-health model for the information ecosystem, with a **4-level prevention framework** directly transferable to our product design:
  - **Primordial** — prevent risk factors for infodemics emerging.
  - **Primary** — literacy-building + **technique-based prebunking** before exposure. *(← our core layer)*
  - **Secondary** — early detection via social listening + **argument-based prebunking**.
  - **Tertiary** — fact-checking/debunking after spread.
  WHO frames "social inoculation" and digital/health literacy as front-line defenses.
- **URLs:** https://www.who.int/health-topics/infodemic · framework discussion (open) https://pmc.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/articles/PMC11369959/ · WHO competency framework for infodemic management https://www.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/pmc/articles/PMC9077350/
- **Adapt into modules:** Use the **4-level model as our product architecture diagram** (prebunk = primary/secondary; debunk tools = tertiary). Gives a WHO-backed rationale for leading with prebunking. Health-rumor examples (vaccines, "natural cures") are highly relevant and emotionally salient in Egypt — build a **health-misinformation track** on this.
- **License/usability:** 🟡 WHO materials generally reusable under CC BY-NC-SA 3.0 IGO (non-commercial) — check per-document; cite WHO. The PMC framework papers are 🟢 open.

### 3.3 Cochrane / GRADE — Evidence Hierarchy

- **What it offers:** The authoritative way to rank *how much to trust a health/science claim*:
  - **Evidence pyramid** (top→bottom): systematic reviews/meta-analyses of RCTs → individual RCTs → cohort studies → case-control → case series/reports → expert opinion.
  - **GRADE** rates certainty of a body of evidence as **High / Moderate / Low / Very Low**, downgrading on 5 domains: risk of bias, inconsistency, indirectness, imprecision, publication bias.
- **URLs:** GRADE at Cochrane https://www.cochrane.org/learn/courses-and-resources/cochrane-methodology/grade · Handbook ch.14 https://www.cochrane.org/authors/handbooks-and-manuals/handbook/current/chapter-14
- **Adapt into modules:** A **"how strong is this evidence?" module** — teach users a simplified pyramid so "a study showed…" stops being a magic phrase. Pair with a heuristic: *one study ≠ proof; ask for the review.* Directly counters health/diet/miracle-cure misinformation common in Arabic social media.
- **License/usability:** 🟢/🟡 The *concepts* (pyramid, GRADE levels) are freely teachable and standard; Cochrane Handbook text is copyrighted — teach the framework, write our own explanations/graphics. Cite Cochrane/GRADE Working Group.

### 3.4 Verification Handbook (European Journalism Centre / DataJournalism.com)

- **What it offers:** Practical, step-by-step verification of social media, images, video, accounts, bots, deepfakes, and information operations. Editor: Craig Silverman. Multiple editions incl. "For Disinformation and Media Manipulation."
- **Critical for us — AVAILABLE FREE IN ARABIC:** Downloadable in **English, Arabic, Turkish, Italian, German**.
- **URLs:** https://datajournalism.com/read/handbook/verification-3 · http://verificationhandbook.com/ · Arabic availability noted via IJNet https://ijnet.org/en/story/free-handbook-verifying-social-media-breaking-news-now-available-arabic
- **Adapt into modules:** Source for the **hands-on "verify this image/video" tier** (reverse image search, geolocation, metadata, deepfake tells). The existing Arabic edition means terminology is already localized — align our wording with it.
- **License/usability:** 🟡 Free to read/download; published by EJC with funder support. Reuse beyond personal/educational use — check EJC terms; cite editor/EJC.

| **Localization / examples / partners** | Da Begad, Fatabyyano, Misbar, AFP Arabic, ARIJ/AFCN | 🔴 cite/partner |

**Three design mandates that fall out of the evidence:**
1. **Teach techniques, not individual hoaxes** — only technique-based ("logic") prebunking generalizes and scales (Jigsaw, *Science Advances* 2022; Lu et al. 2023). This is the spine.
2. **Build discernment, not blanket distrust** — meta-analytic caveat: some interventions just make people say "false" more often (criterion shift). Assess and reward *accuracy on true AND false items* (signal-detection critique, 2025).
3. **Lead with emotion + active role-play + teach-it-forward** — the mechanics with the strongest persistence/transfer evidence (Bad News role-play; IREX emotion + diffusion, effects lasting ~1.5 yrs).

**Documented gap = our opportunity:** No major prebunking instrument has been validated in Arabic / Egyptian-dialect contexts (cross-cultural Bad News covered Swedish/German/Greek/Polish only). An Arabic-validated, Egypt-grounded prebunking curriculum would be a genuine, citable first.
