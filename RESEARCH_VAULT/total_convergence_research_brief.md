## 1. THE TWO-GATE MODEL (كيف نفرّق + كيف نعرف إنه مفيد)

Every candidate case passes **two independent gates, in order**. Failing either = reject or flag, never silently include.

```
LEAD ──▶ GATE 1: RESEARCH STRENGTH ──▶ GATE 2: PROJECT RELEVANCE ──▶ corpus
         (هل البحث قوي؟)                 (هل مفيد للمشروع تحديدًا؟)
         fail → drop/CONTESTED          fail → "strong but out of context" → drop
```

The user's core worry is **Gate 2**: a case can be impeccably sourced (strong) yet useless to us because it is not Egyptian, not multi-layer, or teaches no defense. Both gates are mandatory.

## 2. GATE 1 — STRONG vs WEAK RESEARCH (بحث قوي مقابل بحث ضعيف)

Score each dimension 0–3. **A case needs ≥ 14/21 AND no zero in Sourcing or Cross-Verification** to pass.

| # | Dimension | WEAK (0) | STRONG (3) | Anchored in |
|---|-----------|----------|------------|-------------|
| 1 | **Sourcing tier** | Anonymous FB/WhatsApp screenshot, single blog, AI-generated text | ≥1 Tier S/A primary (academic, CAPMAS, WHO, court ruling, Reuters/AFP) + resolvable URL | Std §3 whitelist |
| 2 | **Cross-verification** | One source repeated | ≥2–3 **independent** sources agree; if AI used, ≥2 providers concur | Std §2 Strategy 3 |
| 3 | **Provenance / traceability** | Cannot trace to origin | Traced to origin; archived (Wayback/hash); quote → full original | Std §2 Strategy 2 |
| 4 | **Micro-data precision** | Vague ("many died") | Names, dates, exact numbers, documented damage | study_the_problem §0 |
| 5 | **Longitudinal depth** | Single snapshot (today/yesterday) | Tracked **≥12 months**: origin → escalation → peak → aftermath → current | NEW (user) |
| 6 | **Independence / cui bono** | Source's incentive ignored | Source bias mapped; beneficiary identified | Std §12 L4 |
| 7 | **Honest abstention** | Gaps hidden, certainty faked | Unknowns labelled; thin claims → CONTESTED/UNVERIFIED | Std §0, §9 |

> **Weak example (reject):** "A viral WhatsApp message says a sheikh cured cancer with honey." One anonymous vector, no origin, no numbers, no timeline.
> **Strong example (pass):** A named figure, dated launch, a peer-reviewed/press-documented harm trail, archived posts, tracked across a year, with the beneficiary and money flow identified.

## 3. GATE 2 — PROJECT RELEVANCE (مفيد للمشروع، مش بحث قوي في غير سياقه)

A strong case is **rejected** unless it clears these. Score and require **all five sub-tests true**.

### 3.1 Geography filter — **EGYPT FIRST, ARAB ONLY TO SUPPLEMENT**
Priority order (hard rule):
1. **EGYPT-SPECIFIC** (default target): Egyptian actor, Egyptian victims, Egyptian platform/market, or Egyptian-dialect spread. → **always eligible.**
2. **PAN-ARAB**: only as *supplement* when it has a clear Egyptian transmission vector or strengthens an Egyptian case. → **eligible only as support.**
3. **GLOBAL**: only if it is the **canonical archetype** of a layer (e.g. Tobacco Playbook, Cambridge Analytica) AND we attach its Egyptian parallel. → **rare, must be tied to Egypt.**

> If a case is global and has **no** Egyptian thread, it is "strong but out of context" → **drop.**

### 3.2 Layer fit
Maps onto ≥1 of the 8 layers (Std §5). **Preference ladder:** all-8 *Total Convergence* ▶ multi-layer (≥3) ▶ single-layer. Single-layer cases only fill explicit corpus gaps.

### 3.3 Cross-domain impact (the user's "تأثيرات متغيرة")
Demonstrable effect on **≥1**, ideally several, of: **Religion · Science/Health · Economy · Politics · Society**. Score each 0–3 (see §6 matrix). High total = high value (it proves the damage is systemic).

### 3.4 Severity / documented harm
Real damage: deaths, money lost, treatment refusal, institutional trust collapse, radicalization. (This is what proves "الوضع سيء جدًا".) No documented harm → educational only, low priority.

### 3.5 Defense teachability
We can attach a **layer defense protocol** (Std §5) and a **specific cognition technique** (Std §12). A case we cannot defend against teaches nothing → deprioritize.

> **Corpus-gap check:** cross against the existing **118 cases** in `src/components/six-layers/data.ts` (exported to `exports/six-layers-cases.json`). Skip duplicates; prefer net-new Egyptian cases.
